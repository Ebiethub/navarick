## Purpose

This document is the source of truth for NAVARICK's website messaging. It defines the approved working copy, hierarchy, alternatives, and voice rules that should be used during final Figma production and implementation.

Project names, case-study narratives, testimonials, outcomes, response times, pricing language, and client claims remain content dependencies. They must be verified before publication.

# 1. Brand Positioning

NAVARICK is a premium creative studio specialising in brand identity, web design, web development, and UI/UX design.

We partner with ambitious businesses to create distinctive brands and digital experiences that build trust, strengthen perception, and support meaningful growth.

## Positioning principles

- NAVARICK is a creative studio, not a generic agency or consultancy.
- Strategy, design, and technology form one coherent practice.
- The work should communicate trust, clarity, distinction, and lasting value.
- Claims should be supported by visible work, client evidence, or measurable outcomes.
- Copy should remain specific and composed rather than inflated.

# 8. Tone of Voice

## Use

- Clear
- Confident
- Professional
- Premium
- Human
- Strategic

## Avoid

- “Innovative solutions”
- “Cutting-edge technology”
- “Transforming businesses”
- “Disruptive creativity”
- Generic agency buzzwords

## Writing principles

### Clear before clever

The visitor should understand the message on the first read. Wordplay must never obscure the service or outcome.

### Specific over inflated

Name the problem, decision, artefact, or result. Avoid unqualified superlatives.

### Compact and composed

Headlines should usually contain 3–10 words. Body paragraphs should usually remain under 70 words.

### Confident, not self-congratulatory

Let work, process, and evidence demonstrate quality. Avoid repeatedly describing NAVARICK as exceptional, world-class, or award-winning.

### Human, not casual

Use direct language and contractions where they sound natural. Avoid corporate abstraction and over-familiar sales language.

### Outcome-aware

Connect design to trust, clarity, usability, recognition, and growth only where the relationship is credible and supportable.

---

# 9. Editorial Style

- Use sentence case for navigation, headings, buttons, and service names.
- Use British English: specialising, colour, optimisation.
- Prefer **studio** to **agency**.
- Use **we** sparingly; lead with the client's need or the work.
- Use an ampersand only where the approved headline or compact discipline label requires it.
- Use an em dash without spaces for a deliberate sentence break.
- Use the serial comma where it improves clarity.
- Avoid exclamation marks in standard website copy.
- Do not use emoji in interface or editorial copy.
- Use numerals for project years, metrics, process indices, and practical ranges.

---

# 10. Content Integrity Rules

- Project outcomes must identify their source and timeframe where relevant.
- Testimonials require name, role, organisation, and approval.
- Conceptual work is labelled as conceptual.
- Do not present collaborators as employees or clients as retained relationships without confirmation.
- Awards, recognition, and client logos require evidence and permission.
- Service lists include only services NAVARICK is prepared to sell and deliver.
- Growth, conversion, trust, and perception claims must be framed as goals unless project evidence proves them as results.
- Placeholder copy is never published accidentally.

# 11. Content Dependencies and Downstream Reflow

## Hero headline

The current primary headline—**Building Brands People Remember.**—replaces the earlier working headline **We give ambitious ideas distinctive form.**

Before final Figma approval:

- update hero line breaks at 1440, 1280, 1024, 768, 390, and 320 px;
- re-check the relationship between headline and hero object;
- retain the earlier “Form, revealed” concept as visual direction unless explicitly retired.

## Hero CTA order

The content system makes **Start a Project** primary and **View Selected Work** secondary. Earlier UX documents placed work discovery first.

This is a conversion-strategy decision that requires explicit approval before design handoff. Recommended default for a portfolio-led first visit remains:

- Primary: View Selected Work
- Secondary: Start a Project

For referral or high-intent campaign traffic, the order may be reversed.

## Process model

The content system defines five stages:

1. Discover
2. Strategy
3. Design
4. Develop
5. Launch

Earlier system documents define four stages: Discover, Define, Design, Deliver.

Choose one model before final Figma design. The five-stage model is more explicit about development and launch; the four-stage model is more concise and applies more naturally to identity-only projects.

## Results language

The phrase **measurable impact** should remain conditional until actual project evidence is available. Where evidence is qualitative, use **meaningful impact** or state the specific observed change.
